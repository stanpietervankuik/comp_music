Conclusion

Musical Spotlights

Song specific feature analyses show that individual songs can reveal aspects of the corpus that are not immediately visible in the broader statistical overview. By focusing on outliers and specific case studies, I was able to connect Spotify feature values to more concrete musical properties such as timbre, harmony, tempo, and tonal change. This makes the broader patterns in the portfolio more tangible, while also showing that individual tracks often contain more complexity than a single feature value can express.

Corpus Wide Analysis

Overall, the classification results support the main idea of this portfolio: my listening behavior does seem to differ between winter and summer, but the strength and nature of that difference depend on the genre. The clearest seasonal distinction appears in Dance, where the classifier performs best and where tempo and energy emerge as the dominant features. This suggests that my winter and summer dance playlists differ mainly in speed and intensity, with winter dance tending to be slightly faster and more energetic.

For Chill, the classifier also performs reasonably well, though less strongly than for Dance. Here, the distinction is driven mainly by valence and speechiness, which fits the earlier corpus exploration. This suggests that the difference between winter and summer chill is less about intensity and more about mood and vocal character: summer chill appears more positive and consistent, while winter chill is more varied and extends further toward darker and more speech-like material.

For Hip Hop, the classification performance is only slightly above chance, which suggests that the winter-versus-summer distinction is much weaker than in the other genres. At the very least, this indicates that the Spotify track-level features used here do not capture a clear seasonal difference for hip hop. This could mean either that such a difference is largely absent, or that it is present in aspects of the music not well represented by these particular features.

Course Reflection

This section may seem a little sentimental, but this was by far my favorite course of the bachelor, so I felt it was worth saying explicitly. I have been producing music for quite some time, and being able to connect that personal interest to academic work was extremely enjoyable. Working on the portfolio never really felt like an obligation; rather, it felt like an opportunity to explore my own music collection in a completely new way.

I even caught myself spending far more time than planned trying to create just one more visualization or explore one more idea, despite the fact that I was also preparing for three other exams that same week. That was not always the most practical choice, but it does say something about how engaging I found the course.

Overall, I would really like to thank you for designing such an enjoyable and inspiring course. It was one of the few courses in which academic work felt closely connected to something I genuinely care about, and that made the whole experience especially valuable.
