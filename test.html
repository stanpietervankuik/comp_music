Computational Musicology

About me

I am Stan Pieter van Kuik, a student at the University of Amsterdam. This page presents my portfolio for the course Computational Musicology.

Portfolio background

Over the last couple of years, I have noticed that my listening behavior seems to be divided quite strongly by season, especially between summer and winter. I also tend to organize my favorite kinds of music into separate playlists, mainly dance music, hip hop, and chill music that I listen to in order to relax. This made me curious whether that seasonal pattern is actually reflected in the music itself, or whether it is mainly just a feeling.

For this portfolio, I built a corpus based on my own listening behavior. The corpus consists of six playlists: separate winter and summer playlists for dance, hip hop, and chill music. This creates a clear structure for comparison across both season and genre, while keeping the corpus closely connected to music that is personally meaningful to me.

To explore this corpus in a structured way, I will first examine the playlists at the level of individual tracks. This begins with a general corpus exploration using Spotify track-level features, after which I will look more closely at chroma features, loudness, timbre features, and temporal features. After these feature-specific analyses, I will return to the corpus as a whole through classification, regression, and clustering, before concluding with a broader reflection on what I learned from the analysis.
